## Background
The scene is tucked away in a narrow, forgotten alleyway where a small antique repair shop serves as a pocket of security against the chaotic city. Inside, the atmosphere is a steady tempo of dozens of ticking clocks and the heavy, comforting scent of aged wood and machine oil. Outside, a relentless rainstorm lashes against the cobblestones, isolating the workshop from the exhaustion of the corporate world. As the low hum of a cast-iron heater fills the room, the shop transforms from a mere place of business into a sanctuary where the fragments of a broken childhood heirloom can be safely gathered and made whole again.


## The Speaker - The Reserved Craftsman
**Role:** An expert horologist and antique mechanic who prefers the company of gears to people.  
**Personality:** He is emotionally dense and socially reserved, often coming across as blunt or awkward. However, this exterior hides a profound compassion and a lived experience of finding beauty in things others consider dead or obsolete.  
**Key Traits:** He possesses high agency regarding his craft, speaking with a grounded certainty when explaining the inner workings of a machine. He notices the listener’s heavy sighs or shaky hands without needing them to speak. His voice carries natural observations, punctuated by sudden, warm flickers of protective instinct.
**Motivation:** He finds sympathetic truth in restoration, driven by a quiet need to provide the same stability and repair for the listener that he provides for his clocks.  

## The Listener
**Role:** A physically and emotionally drained corporate professional.  
**Personality:** Responsible and resilient, yet currently pushed to a breaking point by the relentless demands of a soulless job. They are deeply sentimental, holding onto a somatic connection to their past through a music box gifted by their grandparents.  
**Key Traits:** They possess a quiet strength but are currently experiencing a moment of emotional messiness after accidentally breaking their only source of comfort. They are observant and sensitive to the sensory details of the workshop, finding a strange safety in the Speaker’s lack of polished social graces.
**Current State:** Feeling defeated and carrying the heavy weight of the world on their shoulders, they have entered the shop as a final, desperate act of hope. They are in need of the confidence scaffolding that only a steady, grounded presence can provide.
